This page contains resources and talks that I find inspirational and informative. It is important as people in the field of computing that we understand our roots, so we can understand that there are many ways to approach computing, and what passes for 'best practice' in our field is very often just dogma.

Bret Victor
Inventing on Principle.

Simply the best talk I've seen in the last few years. 		Richard Hamming
You and your research

Hamming is both a genius and a masterful communicator, and his work on coding theory is indispensable. This lecture is not technical, rather it is about adopting a mindset of excellence.
Alan Kay
Normal Considered Harmful

Dr Kay is the man who is both most visionary about how computers ought to behave in concert with man, and the most cynical about how the industry is today. The combination is delightful. 		Hal Abelson
Structure and Interpretation of Computer Programs.
